About Me

Krystal Wang, M.A., is a Data Analyst for the R01 YRBS Project with the Evaluation, Data Integration and Technical Assistance (EDIT) Program at Northwestern University. I received my B.A.s with honors in Psychology and Econometrics and Quantitative Economics at the University of Rochester and recently earned my M.A. in the Social Sciences at the University of Chicago, concentrating on psychology. My past research has focused on analyzing health disparities among marginalized populations and evaluating trends over time to inform interventions in clincal settigns and healthcare policies. My current research interests are intersectionality, suicide and non-suicidal self-injury and alcohol use among racial, gender and sexual minority adolescents. I intend to pursue a Ph.D. in clinical psychology in the future. Photography and art are the flavors of my life.

R01 DAO53250

Marijuana Legalization and Substance Use Among Racial, Ethnic, and Sexual Minorities PI: Renee Johnson, Ph.D., Gregory Phillips II, Ph.D.

This project examines whether marijuana legalization is associated with increases in substance use and associated co-morbidities among Black, Latinx, and sexual minority adolescents using the pooled Youth Risk Behavior Survey (YRBS).

R01 AA029044

Intersectional Approaches to Population-Level Health Research: Role of HIV Risk and Mental Health in Alcohol Use Disparities among Diverse Sexual Minority Youth PI: Gregory Phillips II, Ph.D.

This project examines jurisdictional and temporal associations with alcohol use and disparities incorporating an intersectional lens and the impact of structural level factors that influence alcohol use and other health outcomes in diverse sexual minority youth.

R21 AA028385

Health Disparities among Transgender Students in the United States: Results from the Youth Risk Behavior Survey (YRBS) PI: Lauren Beach, Ph.D., Sari Reisner, Ph.D.

This project evaluates disparities in health by transgender identity among U.S. youth in partnership with the Center for Disease Control and leading transgender health researchers.

R01 AA024409

Role of Alcohol Disparities in HIV Risk among Sexual Minority Youth PI: Gregory Phillips II, Ph.D.)

The project examines differences between sexual minority youth and their heterosexual peers on alcohol use and other factors that place them at risk for HIV infection, using the pooled YRBS.
